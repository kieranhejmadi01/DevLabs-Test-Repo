## 🗺️ Overview

AI products can succeed on engagement metrics while still causing user harm, reputational damage, or broader negative outcomes. Developers need repeatable methods to surface unintended consequences before these systems scale.

In this challenge, you apply Yellow Teaming to an Arm-based AI product concept. You can implement the process with a private or public LLM, or carry it out manually, as long as you document the analysis clearly and show how it improves the product.


## 🚀 Mission brief

Introduce a structured Yellow Teaming workflow that stress-tests an AI product by exploring the consequences of success, not only obvious failures.

## 🎯 Suggested scope

Your project can include:

- Detailed accounts of AI projects developed using Yellow Teaming principles on Arm-based systems
- Documentation of unintended consequences and mitigations
- Product or feature changes inspired by the Yellow Teaming analysis
- Demonstrations of Arm-based AI deployment such as AWS Graviton or mobile devices

## 💡 Skills you can practice

- Responsible AI evaluation
- LLM-assisted analysis workflows
- Product design review on Arm-based AI systems
- Documentation of safety and societal impact decisions

## ✅ Assessment criteria

Submissions are assessed using broad criteria that reflect the quality of the analysis, the usefulness of the outcomes, and the relevance to Arm-based AI systems.

    {{< tab header="Depth of analysis" >}}
Assessors look for thoughtful exploration of downstream consequences rather than a surface-level list of risks. Strong submissions identify realistic success cases, secondary effects, and practical mitigations that teams could act on.

    {{< tab header="Quality of recommendations" >}}
Your project should turn analysis into useful design or product guidance. Strong entries connect findings to concrete changes in workflow, product behavior, safety policy, or deployment decisions.

- Access to an Arm-based cloud instance if you host a private model
- Access to a public LLM if you prefer a hosted workflow
